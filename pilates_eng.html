Classic pilates

Classical Pilates exercises are exercises for strength, mobility and flexibility of the whole body. The emphasis is on lengthening the muscles, so he gained on his grace, and the body on flexibility. Exercises are repeated slowly, controlled with proper breathing. Pilates largely avoids high impact, high power output, and heavy muscular and skeletal loading. core. These are the muscles of the abdomen, back, as well as the pelvis, which, as the central part of the body, give us stability and strength, giving support to the whole body.

Stott pilates


25min
3/5
5/5
Stott Pilates is the most respected international school of modern Pilates, built on the principles of classical learning and upgraded with new knowledge from the theory of training and kinesitherapy. The emphasis of this method is on preserving the natural curve of the spine, stabilizing the center of the body and the shoulder girdle, neutral positioning of the joints and proper breathing. Balancing between muscle strength and flexibility. Stott pilates will safely improve your posture, reduce back pain and tension in the pelvic muscles. With this kind of training, you will build a body of slender muscle tone, enviable strength and mobility, and above all, a healthy spirit.

Reformer pilates


40min
5/5
4/5
Today, there are a large number of different Pilates techniques, and one of them is practicing on the Reformer device, which is a revolutionary invention and has been a hit in the world and Europe for years. Pilates device - The reformer consists of a movable platform that slides back and forth on the sliders and uses springs as resistances. We activate the mobile platform by pulling the rope. Many professional athletes train at the Reformer, and he helped some to return to the game as soon as possible after injuries. It can be adapted to any exerciser, regardless of height, weight, mobility and ability.

Therapeutic pilates


45min
5/5
4/5
Therapeutic Pilates is based on the basic principles of Stott Pilates (see Stott Pilates), but since it is intended for those who have problems in the back and neck as well as in the knee, hip and shoulder joint, it also has its peculiarities. Technique modified for maximum protection and efficiency in patients with spinal diseases. There are basic Pilates principles of connection between breathing and movement, precision, harmony of movements, centering (strengthening the center of the body), stability, range of motion. The essence is in strengthening the musculature of the "center of the body", that is. the abdominal muscles of the back and pelvis, which are responsible for maintaining good posture and the health of the spine.
